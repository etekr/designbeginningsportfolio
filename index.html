Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

This course requires us to think critically about the process of making things, specifically things that are meant to communicate something. Why are we making what we make, who is it for, what tools can I use to convey my message, who will benefit from this? These are these roles manifest and what we consider when designing.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

We leverage design principles by not simply considering the things we personally like and think look “good” design. We apply concepts we have learned through readings and practice in projects to create designs that actually serve the design and its message over our own interests.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

We discuss our progess in our projects with our peers every class, which strengthens the comfotablity and curiosity of the environment. It is rewarding to help classmates through their problems and roadblocks, and to feel comfortable to enlist help for your own troubles.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

It is understood that our designs are never truly finished, and if you think it is, you're probably wrong. Any good design should be tried, tested, revisited, and looked at from every possible angle before it can truly be considered done.

5. Learn to present your work.

Designs do not exist in a vacuum; they exist to be seen and interacted with. Presenting our designs to an audience put the class in a realistic scenario where you're explaining and defending the way you conveyed your ideas to a customer or employer. Getting feedback on your work is essential.
